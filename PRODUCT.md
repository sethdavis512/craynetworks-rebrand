# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two audiences, by design:

- **Cray's customers** (the in-world audience): small and mid-sized businesses plus residential clients across Central Texas (Leander, Cedar Park, Austin, Round Rock, Pflugerville, Georgetown, and the Hill Country) who need dependable IT support, network management, hosting, and repair. They are non-technical or semi-technical, evaluating whether this local shop is competent and trustworthy enough to hand their systems to.
- **A design-heavy hiring manager** (the real audience): evaluating the maker's skill as a design engineer. They will poke at interactions, read the code and the git history, and judge typography, token-system architecture, and real-time theming. The site must read as "this person understands how design systems function," demonstrated live.

## Product Purpose

Rebrand Cray Networks from a dated, generic IT-shop site into a warm, credible, modern local-IT partner, and, in the same artifact, demonstrate design-system engineering at a senior level. Success is twofold: the rebrand reads as a believable, better Cray (continuity with their real blue-and-white identity, finally systematized, with a real dark mode), and the build itself functions as a portfolio piece that makes a design-heavy reviewer want to hire the maker.

## Brand Personality

The local IT expert who treats you like a neighbor: warm, human, trustworthy, competent without being cold. Voice is plain-spoken and confident, it invites rather than scolds, and it never flexes jargon to seem smart. Emotional goal: relief and confidence ("these people have us handled"), not enterprise intimidation.

## References

Named, for the specific quality to echo (not category vibes):

- **Linear** for engineered precision, restraint, and taut spacing, the "serious tooling" credibility signal.
- **Kumo (kumo-ui.com)** for component-documentation clarity: live preview over copyable code, clean API tables, calm three-column docs.
- **Coss UI** for its component/design-system presentation.

## Anti-references

- **Generic MSP/IT-shop sites**: stock data-center photos, clip-art icons, corporate gradient blues. This is exactly what Cray's current site is; escaping it is the whole point.
- **Corporate-blue SaaS template**: the navy/blue, bootstrap-flavored template every IT vendor ships.
- **AI-slop tells**: cream/sand backgrounds, per-section uppercase eyebrows, 01/02/03 section markers, identical card grids, gradient text.
- **Cold enterprise dashboards**: sterile, gray, dense admin-panel aesthetics with no warmth or human signal.

## Design Principles

- **Typography carries the brand.** Type is the primary expressive instrument here, not an afterthought. The craft is made tangible and configurable (a live type lab), and hierarchy, rhythm, and reading comfort are treated as first-class. If one thing is unforgettable, it is the typography.
- **The medium is the proof.** The site demonstrates design-system mastery live rather than claiming it. The token system, the instant theming, and the git history are the argument; nothing is asserted that the build does not show.
- **Warm expertise.** Competent without being cold. Invite, do not scold. Clarity and confidence over intimidation.
- **Continuity over reinvention.** Enhance Cray's existing blue-and-white identity rather than replacing it. The thesis is "same brand, finally systematized," which makes continuity a feature, not a constraint.
- **Accessibility is engineered, not hoped for.** Contrast, reduced motion, keyboard and screen-reader support, and spatial-UI fallbacks are provable, not aspirational.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA, verified with APCA readouts, in both light and dark themes (dark mode is first-class, not an afterthought). Honor `prefers-reduced-motion` across every animation system. Full keyboard and screen-reader support, including a semantic non-spatial fallback for the `/explore` canvas. Mobile-first and responsive throughout.
